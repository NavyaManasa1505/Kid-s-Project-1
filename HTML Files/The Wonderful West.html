Let's explore some cities in western India.

MUMBAI

Mumbai is the largest city situated on the west coast of India. Earlier, it was called Bombay. Mumbai is the capital of Maharashtra. Mumbai is home to the world's largest film industry.

Climate: In summer, Mumbai is warm and humid. As it is on the coast of the Arabian sea. The sea makes the weather in Mumbai very humid most of the year. From June to September, there is heavy rainfall. At times, the rainwater rises to knee level or even higher. Between November and February, it is cool and pleasant.

People: In Mumbai, people speak Marathi, Bhelpuri, vada pav, pani puri and pav bhaji are popular snacks. Ganesh Chaturthi is a very important festival in Mumbai. People especially celebrate all other festivals. Mumbai has many big offices where a lot of people work. The sea is a huge source of oil. The place where the oilfields are located is called the Mumh High. There are many factories in and around Mumbai. The cloth industry is One of the most important industries there.

Transport: Mumbai is well-connected by a network of transport system. One of the grandest buildings of Mumbai is also one of India's busiest ralway stations, named the Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST). Local trains connect the different parts of this big city. Buses are a common means of travel. Some of the buses are even double-decker or have two storeys. The Sahara International Airport in Mumbai is one of the busiest airports in India.

Tourist Attraction: The most famous tourist spot is the Gateway of India. Other places to see are Kamla Nehru Park, Marine Drive, Taraporewala Aquarium and the Essel world. Being near the coast, Mumbai has some wonderful beaches. Chowpatty, Juhu and Versova are among the most famous. The Elephanta Caves near Mumbai have many rock-cut temples. The best view of Mumbai can be had from the Malabar Hills. At nights, lights along the Marine Drive look like a necklace. That is why this stretch is also called the Queen's Necklace.

AHMEDABAD

Ahmedabad is the largest city in Gujarat. It is located along the banks of River Sabarmati. Ahmedabad is also known as Amdavad. It is the fifth most populous city in India.

Climate: Summers are very hot and dry. In the winter months, Ahmedabad experiences a very dry climate. The climate is very pleasant and comfortable during the time. It rains heavily from July to September. During this time weather and climate in Ahmedabad is humid.

People: People of Ahmedabad, are very lively and cherish in each and every moment of their lives. People speak Gujarati and Hindi. Dhokla, fafda, khaman, khandvi and khakhra are some popular snacks. All major festivals are celebrated here. During Navaratri, people perform Raas Garba a folk dance of Gujarat.

Tourist Attraction: One of the most popular tourist attractions is Sabarmati Ashram, also known as Gandhi Ashram. This is a place that holds historical importance. Some other places of tourist interest are Jhulta Minar, Kankaria lake, Kite Museum, Autoworld Vintage Car Museum and science city.

JAIPUR

Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthan. The city was founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh. Most buildings in the old city are made of pink sandstone. That is why Jaipur is popularly known as Pink city. It is situated in the eastern part of Rajasthan. Jaipur is famous for its handicrafts such as jewellery metal work, tie and dye and ivory work. Johri Bazar is one of the main shopping centres in the City.

Climate: Jaipur has a hot semi-arid climate. The city remains quite hot and dry during the summers and is pleasant during winters. The best season to visit Jaipur is between October to March. During these months, the weather is mild and pleasant.

People: People speak Hindi and English. In general, the people of Jaipur are fond of their folk culture comprising of dance and singing. Rajasthani folklore is the traditional song of Jaipur. Different dances are seen like Ghumar, Chari etc. Traditional instruments of this area are Sarangi, Ektara, Jhalar and Surbahar.

Tourist Attraction: Jaipur is part of the heritage of India and attracts a large number of tourists from all over the world. The Hawa Mahal, City Palace, Rambagh Palace, Amber Fort and the Jantar Mantar are some places visited by tourists.

BHOPAL

Bhopal is the capital of Madhya Pradesh, situated along the banks of two lakes. Upper lake and the Lower lake. It is also known as the city of lakes. Bhopali is famous for its jewellery and handicrafts.

Climate: Summers are very hot and dry. Winters are very cold. It rains heavily from June to September. Although Bhopal is beautiful during monsoon.

People: People speak Hindi, Urdu and English. All major festivals are celebrated in the city.

Tourist Attraction: Some famous places that tourists like to visit are Moti Masjid, Taj-ul-Masjid, Ekant Park, Van Vihar National Park and Bharat Bhawan.

Let's see the cities of eastern india.

KOLKATA

Kolkata is the capital of West Bengal. Earlier, it was called Calcutta. It is situated along the banks of River Hugli. It is a major part. The hanging bridge on the Hugli was called Howrah Bridge. It is now called Rabindra Setu. Another bridge has been built on the river. It is called Vidyasagar Setu.

Climate: Kolkata is neither hot nor cold. It receives heavy rainfall between June and September. The weather is warm and humid during these months.

People: Most men wear dhoti-kurta or shirt-trousers. Women wear sarees salwar-kameez, jeans and skirts. Bangla is the main language. Most people eat rice. Fish curry and rasogulla are popular food items. All major festivals are celebrated in the city. Durga Puja is the main festival. People also like to watch cricket and football. There are many industries in Kolkata, Jute silk, rice, paper and chemicals are a few important industries.

Tourist Attraction: There are many places to see in the city. Victoria Memorial Fort William Birla Planetarium, Alipur Zoo, Nicco Park, Rabindra Sarobar, Belur Math and science city are some popular places tourists like to see.

BHUBANESHWAR

Bhubaneshwar also known as the City of Temples, is the capital of Odisha. It is situated on the eastern coastal plains. The city is bounded by the Daya River to the south and the Kuakhai River to the east. Bhubaneshwar is famous for its stonework, bell metalwork, wood carving and pattachitra painting.

Climate: The summer months from March to May are hot and humid. The winter in Bhubaneshwar is chilly, but not extremely cold. The monsoon season starts in July and lasts till October. During mansoon, the weather is quite pleasant.

People: The people of Bhubaneshwar are mainly rural people. Odia is the main language of Bhubaneshwar. As the temple city of Bhubaneshwar is a holy and religious place, people perform several ceremonies as a mark of respect towards their deities.

Tourist Attraction: Some famous places that tourists like to visit are Pathani Samanta Planetarium, Nandankanan Zoo, the Odisha State Museum and Kanjia lake. Khandagiri and Udayagiri are famous for their rock-cut caves.

GUWAHATI

Guwahati is the biggest city in Assam. It is situated along the banks of River Brahmaputra. Guwahati is famous for its handicraft and woollen shawls.

Climate: The climate in Guwahati is warm and temperate. The summer months from March to June and quite hot and humid. It rains heavily from June to September. During winters, the weather is cool and pleasant. The days are sunny and mornings are misty.

People: Assamese is the main language. People of Guwahati follow different types of religions like Buddhism, Sikhism, Christianity and Muslims. Rice is the main part of the food in Guwahati. Bihu is the main festival celebrated in the city. The main occupation of the Guwahati people is weaving.

Tourist Attraction: Some places of tourist interest are the Kamakhya temple, State Museum, Srimanta Sankaradev Kalakshetra and the Forest Museum. Another tourist attraction is Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary. It is a wildlife reserve situated in the Morigaon district.

RANCHI

Ranchi is the capital of Jharkhand. It is located on the Chota Nagpur plateau. The Subarnarekha river flows through the city. The city is famous for musical instruments, jewellery, handicrafts such as metalwork, bamboo craft and paitkar painting.

Climate: Ranchi has a humid sub tropical climate. The summer months are very hot and humid. The winters are cold. It rains heavily from June to September.

People: The major religion is Hinduism followed by Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism and Jainism. The tribal people follow their own religion (Sarna Dharam). Most people speak Hindi. All major festivals are celebrated here. One of the important festivals is Sarhul festival.

Tourist Attraction: There are many waterfalls near Ranchi such as Dassam falls, Hundru falls, Sita falls and Jonha falls. Ranchi is also known as the City of Waterfalls. The Nakshatra Van, Tagore Hill, Sun Temple and Rock Garden are some places popular with tourists.

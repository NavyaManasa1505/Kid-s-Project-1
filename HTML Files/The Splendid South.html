CHENNAI

Till 1996, Chennai was called Madras. In 1996, the city changed its name to Chennai. Chennai is located on the southeast coast of India facing the Bay of Bengal. Chennai is the capital of Tamil Nadu. It is an important port.

Climate: The climate of Chennai is warm and humid, almost throughout the year. It gets really hot during the summer months of April and May. The areas near the beach enjoy the sea breeze. The winter is not very cold. It usually rains during winter.

People: People speak Tamil and English. Here, people usually wear cotton clothes throughout the year. People wear both modern and traditional clothes. Man wear veshti (dhoti) and shirt. They also wear trousers. Women wear sarees. During festivals, girls wear a pavadai which is a long skirt. Rice is the main food. It is eaten with sambhar, rasam and curd. Idli, Dosa, Vada, appam and uttapam are some popular snacks in Chennai. All the main festivals are celebrated here. Pongal, a harvest festival is celebrated in January. The famous dance of Tamil Nadu is Bharatnatyam. People in Chennai love music and dance.

Tourist Attraction: Chennai is an important tourist centre. The main attraction of the city is its beaches-the Marina beach and Edward Elliot's beach. Other popular tourist places are the War Memorial, Kapaleeshwarar Temple Fort St. George, St. Thomas Church, Kapaleeshwarar Temple and Government Museum. Like other cities, Chennai also has many malls.

BENGALURU

Bengaluru, the Garden city is the capital of Karnataka, I Was earlier called Bangalore. It is located in southern India on the Deccan plateau. It is the third most populous city in India. Bengaluru has many industries such as aircraft, textiles, chemicals and electronic goods. Many computer companies have their offices here. Bengaluru is also known as the Silicon Valley of India.

Climate: The climate of Bengaluru is moderate throughout the year. Because of frequent rainfalls and thunderstorms, the temperature remains mostly comfortable during the summer months. The winter months from December to February are pleasant with the occasional chilly evenings. The best time to visit the city is during the months of October to February.

People: The people of Bengaluru are called Bangalorean. One of the major religions of Bengalore is Hinduism. All the main festivals are celebrated here. Dussehra is the most important festival. Kannada is the official language of Karnataka and the most widely spoken here.

Tourist Attraction: Some places of tourists interest are Lalbagh, Tipu Sultan's Palace, Government Museum and Acrospace Museum. The Vidhana Soudha is the famous landmark. Another tourists attraction is Cubbon park. It is locatedin the heart of the city.

HYDRABAD

Hyderabad is the capital of Telangana. It is also known as the city of Pearls. It is the fourth most populous city in India. Secunderabad is located close to Hyderabad. These two cities, connected by the Hussain Sagar lake are known as the twin cities. Hyderabad is one of the largest metropolitan areas in India. Hyderabad is famous for its sarees and lac bangles. The city is also an important centre of the computer industry.

Climate: The summer months from April to June are hot and humid. In winter months from December to January are the coldest. It rains heavily from June to September.

People: The people of Hyderabad are called Hyderabadis. One of the major religion is Hinduism followed by Muslims, who speak mostly Telugu, Hindi and Urdu.

Tourist Attraction: The Charminar is the signature of Hyderabad as the Taj Mahal is of Agra. It was built by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1591. Some other places that tourists like to visit are Golconda Fort, Chowmahalla Palace, Salar Jung Museum, Mecca Masjid and Laad Bazaar.

VISAKHAPATNAM

Visakhapatnam is the largest city of Andhra Pradesh. It is popularly known as Vizag. It is the 9th most populous metropolitan city in India. Vizag is a coastal and port city. It is surrounded by beautiful hills.

Climate: The summer months from March to May are hot and humid. From June to September, the city receives heavy rainfall. The winter months from December to February are quite pleasant.

People: The people of Vizag are very modest and helpful. Telugu is widely spoken by the local people. Fishing is the main occupation of the people. All the festivals are celebrated here but the most famous festivals of Vizag are Rishikonda Beach festival and Visakha Utsav.

Tourist Attraction: Rishikonda Beach is very popular among the people. Dolphin's Nose, INS Kursura Submarine Museum, Beach Road and Indira Gandhi Zoological Park are few other places that tourists visit.

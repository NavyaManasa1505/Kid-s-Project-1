A Symbol is a sign to help us recognize something. Every country has some symbols which tells us about the identity values and culture of that country. Our National flag, National Emblem, National anthem, National animal, National bird and National flower are the symbols of our freedom and unity. Let's find out what they are:

THE NATIONAL FLAG

Our National flag is called a tiranga or the tri-colour, because it has three colours:

  • Saffron stands for courage and sacrifice
  • White stands for truth, peace and unity
  • Green stands for prosperity
In the centre, there is a wheel in navy blue colour with 24 spokes known as the Ashoka Chakra. The flag is always made of Khadi cloth or silk. The national flag should always be respected. It should never touch the ground.

THE NATIONAL EMBLEM

The national emblem is the seal of the Government of India. It can be seen on government papers, currency notes, coins and official letters.

    Features of National Emblem:

  • Our national emblem has been adopted from the Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath.
  • It consist of four Asiatic lions standing back to back on a circular stand. The lion stand back to back symbolize power, courage, pride and confidence
  • The base of emblem has a horse, a bull, an elephant and a lion seperated by wheel. This is also called the lion capital of Ashoka
The words Satyameva Jayate are written below the wheel which means truth alone triumphs.

THE NATIONAL ANTHEM

Our national anthem is Jana Gana Mana. It was composed by Rabindranath Tagore. It was adopted as the National Anthem on 24 January 1950. It is a song in praise of our beloved motherland. We must show respect to our national anthem. We must learn the song and sign it properly. When we sing it or hear it being sung, we must stand in attention. We pay tribute to our country when we sing the national anthem.The national anthem is sung on national days, functions and international events.

THE NATIONAL SONG

Our national song is the Vande Mataram. It was composed by Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay. This song was sung by Rabindranath Tagore during the 1896 session of the Indian National Congress. It proved to be a source of inspiration during the freedom struggle.

THE NATIONAL ANIMAL

The Royal Bengal Tiger is our national animal. It stands for power and majesty. It also symbolizes India's wildlife, wealth and power. To protect tigers, a special project called project Tiger was started by the government. Now a days, tigers are protected in national parks and wildlife sanctuaries.

THE NATIONAL BIRD

The peacock is our national bird. It is beautiful bird with a long blue neck and a tail of colourful feathers. The peacock is found in almost all the parts of India. It is symbol of beauty and grace. It has important place in our art and culture. It is a protected bird and no one is allowed to hunt it.

THE NATIONAL FLOWER

The Lotus is our national flower. It symbolizes purity, grows in muddy ponds and lakes. The lotus is thought to be a sacred flower and has been widely used in our art and culture.

Our national symbols represent the culture & values of our country. If we do not respect our national symbols, we are actually not respecting ourselves.

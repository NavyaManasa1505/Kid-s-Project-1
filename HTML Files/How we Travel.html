INTERNATINAL GAME EVENTS

We need to travel from one place to another all the time for various purposes Such as marriages, functions etc. We can walk to places which are very close. But to go to far places, we have to use a bicycle, rickshaw, scooter, car, bus, train, ship or aeroplane. These are all different means of transport. The vehicle we use depends on the following:

  • location of the place
  • distance to be covered
  • time available
  • money we can spend

There are three types of transport-land, air and water.

LAND TRANSPORT

Some vehicles move on land. They are means of land transport. There are two kinds of land transport road and rail. Bicycles, scooters, motorcycles, cars, trucks and buses move on roads. Trains move on railway tracks. In some cities, people travel by the Metro. Metro trains run on special tracks. In Mumbai, most people use local trains. In Kolkata, some people travel by trams. Bullock carts and tractors are very common in villages. Elephants are used in Forests. Camels pull carts and carry people in deserts. In hilly regions, ponies, donkeys and mules are used as means of transport.

AIR TRANSPORT

An aeroplane is a very fast means of transport. It can cover long distances in a short time. People use it to go from one city or country to another. An aeroplane is also used to send letters and goods. A helicopter can carry a few people. It is often used to take food and medicines to people in times of need. It can reach remote areas.

WATER TRANSPORT

Boats, steamers and ships are important means of water transport. Boats and steamers sail on rivers. Ships sail across oceans and seas. They carry passengers and heavy goods from one country to another. Ships need a good port or harbour for loading and unloading goods. There are 13 major ports in India. Mumbai is the busiest and biggest port in India.

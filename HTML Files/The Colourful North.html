Let's Explore some cities of North India.

DELHI

Delhi is the capital of India and a city with a rich history. It is situated along the banks of River Yamuna. The official name of the Delhi is National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT). Delhi is the capital of NCT. The city was planned by Sir Edwin Lutyens. Delhi shares its border with Uttar Pradesh in the east and with Haryana on the other three sides. Delhi is one of the four metropolitan cities of India.

Climate: Delhi is very hot in summer and very cold in winter. In the summer months of May and June, hot and dry winds called loo blow across the city. During July and August, the rain brings some relief from the heat.

People: In Delhi, one can meet people from different parts of the country. They live and work here. They speak different languages, eat different food, wear different clothes, and celebrate different festivals. Thus, we can call Delhi Mini-India.

Government: The main offices of the Government of India are located in New Delhi. The highest law-making body is also here. It is called the Parliament. The governments of other countries also have their offices in Delhi. These are known as Embassies or High Commissions.

Transport: Delhi has various means of public transport. The Delhi Transport Corporation (DTC) operates thousands of buses. Every day lakhs of people travel by metro.

Tourist Attraction: There and are manymonuments to see in Delhi. The Red Fort the Jama Masjid were built by Shah Jahan. On 15th August, the Prime Ministerof India hoists the national flag at the Red Fort.

  • Red Fort: Many years ago, the Red Fort, a huge red sandstone fort, was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the heart of the city. The fort houses the Diwan-i-aam, a hall once studded with precious stones. Here, the Emperor met his people and tried to solve their problems. The Diwan-i-Khas was the hall for royal guests.

  • Jama Masjid: The Jama Masjid in Delhi is the largest mosque in India. It was bun by Emperor Shah Jahan in red sandstone and white marble. It is a grand place O worship where people have been praying for many years.
Other interesting places to visit are Rai Ghat (Samadhi of Mahatma Gandhi), Jantar Mantar, India Gate, Humayun's Tomb, Qutub Minar, Lodi Gardens, Lotus Temple, Akshardham Temple, and Dilli Haat.

CHANDIGARH

Chandigarh is a city and a union territory in India. It is the capital of two neighboring states-Punjab and Haryana. It is a well-planned city designed by a French architect named Le Corbusier. Chandigarh is divided into many sectors.

Climate: Chandigarh enjoys an extreme climate with hot summers (March to June) and chilly winters (November to February). The monsoon season, though pleasant in the evening is humid during the daytime. The best season to visit Chandigarh is Autumn (August to November).

People: Chandigarh is home to a wide variety of people belonging to different classes. The best thing about the people of Chandigarh is their happy-go-lucky nature. People speak Punjabi, Hindi and English.

Tourist Attraction: Many people visit the famous Rock garden created by Nek Chand. It has colorful people, trees, houses and animals, all made from discarded materials. Another tourist attraction is the Zakir Hussain Rose Garden. It is one of the biggest gardens in Asia. It has more than a thousand varieties of roses. Sukhna Lake is also a famous tourist spot in the city

LUCKNOW

Lucknow is the capital and largest city of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is also known as the city of Nawabs. It is situated along the banks of River Gomti. People speak Hindi, Urdu and English. The Chikan embroidery of Lucknow is very famous. It is done on kurtas, sarees, pillow covers and bedsheets. The city is famous for its mouth-watering dishes such as different varieties of kebabs, kormas and Wangi paranthas.

Climate: The climate in Lucknow is mild and generally warm and template. In winter, there is much less rainfall than in summer. The winters are also marked by mist and fog in the mornings.

Tourist Attraction: Lucknow, the capital of Uttar Pradesh, popularly known as the city of Nawabs is one of the most ancient Hindu states with rich cultural heritage and monuments. Some places that tourists like to see are Bara Imambara, Chot Imambara, the Residency, Rumi Darwaza, Clock Tower, Chattar Manzil and the Shaheed Smarak.

SRINAGAR

Srinagar is the summer capital of Jammu & Kashmir, located in the Kashmir valley, surrounded by the Himalayas on all sides. It is situated on the banks of River Jhelum. It is a popular place among tourists.People drink Kahwa, a special type of tea. A special meal called Wazwan is prepared during weddings and on special occasions.

Climate: Srinagar is very cold in winters. The city receives snowfall in December and January. Summers are generally pleasant. The rainfall in Srinagar is significant.

Tourist Attraction: Mostly, tourists love to visit the Dal lake and the Mughal gardens (Shalimar Bagh). The Dal lake is popular for its Shikara rides. Apple orchards also attract tourists.

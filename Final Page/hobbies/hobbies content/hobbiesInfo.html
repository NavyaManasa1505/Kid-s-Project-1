YOGA

Practicing yoga helps in controlling an individual's mind, body and soul.

And now, how does it help children?

Yoga asanas help kids understand how different body parts work in sync with their breath to get the whole system together. This self-awareness helps them respect, love and take care of their body. The different types of asanas require children to focus and work on their memorization skills which can translate over into their academic performance. GARDENING

Gardening is a great and one of the best activities to do with kids. Children can learn the names of colors, fruits and vegetables. Kids develop ownership and responsibility with gardening. They learn the importance of food and eventually do not waste their food.

Gardening is also a fun way to improve the physical health. From carrying objects to digging and planting, building sheds and just moving stuff around in the yard, children are challenging their muscles' daily.

ORIGAMI

It is the Japanese art of folding paper to create both 2D and 3D figures and decorative shapes.

Origami is a well-suited activity to do with the whole classroom. In a multi-age classroom, irrespective of their age, children learn from each other and eventually,they understand the importance of cooperative learning. It unites the children with similar thoughts and helps in community building.

Transforming a flat piece of paper into a three-dimensional figure is a unique exercise that develops math skills and special reasoning in children. Through hands-on activities, children learn the concept of symmetry and to manipulate basic geometric shapes such as squares, rectangles and triangles.

MARTIAL ARTS

A sport that is originated chiefly in Japan, Korea and China as forms of self-defence or attack.

Almost all types of Martial Arts have a belt or ranking system that children work through, meaning they are rewarded for their practice and mastery of skills. Focusing on a goal such as achieving a new rank or belt is a great way to learn about goal-setting and motivation. Martial arts improves confidence and build self-esteem in children. Continuous and vigorous practice makes the body flexible and improves both mental and physical stamina.
